Suzuki Motor Corporation

MOQBA

Mobility Robot

Suzuki's MOQBA is a modular electric quadruped-wheeled vehicle that climbs stairs and adapts to chair, stand, or stretcher modes.

Overview

Unveiled at the Japan Mobility Show 2023, MOQBA is Suzuki's bold concept for next-generation urban mobility. It blends four electric wheels with robotic legs, enabling movement on flat surfaces and climbing stairs. Designed for accessibility, emergency response, and small-scale transport, this modular prototype can adjust its configuration for seated, standing, or stretcher use.

The innovative design represents a paradigm shift in personal mobility, combining the efficiency of wheeled transport with the adaptability of legged robotics to navigate complex urban environments that traditional vehicles cannot access. MOQBA's transformative approach opens new possibilities for inclusive transportation solutions.

Technical Specifications

Architecture
Four robotic legs, each with 3 degrees of freedom, ending in electric wheels
Riding Modes
Chair Mode (seated driving), Standing Mode (upright control), Stretcher Mode (patient transport)
Mobility
Legs compress and extend to adapt to terrain, with speed bumps and stairs traversal via leg articulation
Top Speed
Approximately 32 km/h (20 mph)
Range
Up to 80 km (50 miles) per charge
Modularity
Interchangeable attachments include wheelchair, cargo basket, robotic arm, or stretcher
Terrain Capability
Flat surfaces, stairs, curbs, and various urban obstacles
Power Source
Electric battery system with regenerative capabilities

Capabilities

Stair-climbing: Ascends and descends steps one leg at a time while keeping passengers level and stable.
Versatile configurations: Transitions smoothly between chair, stand, stretcher, or cargo use for different needs.
Enhanced accessibility: Tailored for elderly, disabled, emergency services, or deliveries in complex urban environments.
Adaptive terrain navigation: Automatically adjusts leg positioning and wheel configuration based on surface conditions.
Modular functionality: Quick-change system for different operational modes and attachments.

Applications

Urban commuting & Last-mile travel: Ideal for navigating curbs and staircases in cities where traditional vehicles can't go.
Accessibility mobility: Empowers those with limited mobility to reclaim independence in challenging environments.
Emergency response: Quickly transport patients or supplies in challenging environments and disaster zones.
Light logistics: Carries goods, groceries, or refuse through built-up areas with stairs and obstacles.
Healthcare support: Mobile assistance platform for medical facilities and home care services.
