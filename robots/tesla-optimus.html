Tesla, Inc.

Tesla Optimus

Humanoid Robot

Tesla Optimus is a humanoid robot designed to perform manual tasks, blending AI with robotics for future labor automation.

Overview

Tesla's Optimus (also known as the Tesla Bot) is a general-purpose humanoid robot under development by Tesla, Inc. It was unveiled by Elon Musk during Tesla's AI Day event in August 2021. Designed to handle tasks that are repetitive, dangerous, or boring for humans, Optimus aims to revolutionize industries by introducing an affordable, autonomous, and safe labor force.

Built with Tesla's expertise in AI, autopilot hardware, and energy systems, the robot represents Tesla's ambition to extend its AI capabilities beyond vehicles. The robot leverages the same advanced neural networks and computer vision systems that power Tesla's Full Self-Driving technology, adapted for bipedal locomotion and manual manipulation tasks.

Technical Specifications

Height
~5 ft 8 in (~1.73 meters)
Weight
~125 pounds (~57 kg)
Payload Capacity
~20 pounds (~9 kg)
Maximum Deadlift
~150 pounds (~68 kg)
Maximum Speed
~5 miles per hour (~8 km/h)
Hands
Human-like, 5 fingers, precision grip
Actuators
28 structural actuators, 11 hand actuators
Sensors
Autopilot cameras, FSD hardware, force feedback sensing, tactile sensors
Power Source
Battery pack (estimated 2.3 kWh)
Runtime
Designed for a full day of work on a single charge
Processor
Tesla's Full Self-Driving Computer (FSD Computer)
Connectivity
Wi-Fi and LTE modules (expected)

Capabilities

Mobility: Walking at a moderate pace, navigating complex environments.
Dexterity: Manipulating objects, using tools, picking up delicate or heavy items.
Perception: Understanding its environment via cameras and sensors (similar to Tesla's vehicle perception stack).
Cognition: Basic decision-making, simple conversations, and task learning through demonstrations.
Safety: Designed with lightweight materials and limited physical power to minimize risk to humans.

Applications

Industrial: Assisting in manufacturing, warehouse logistics, and material handling.
Domestic: Performing household chores, running errands.
Commercial: Serving in retail, restaurants, and customer service roles.
Healthcare: Supporting elderly or disabled individuals with daily activities.
Space and Exploration: Long-term potential to assist in extraterrestrial missions where human labor is costly and risky.
