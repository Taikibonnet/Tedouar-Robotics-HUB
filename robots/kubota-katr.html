Kubota Corporation

KATR

Agricultural Robot
🏆 CES 2025 Best of Innovation

Kubota's KATR is a compact, hydraulically legged all-terrain robot offering stable cargo transport across slopes and rugged environments.

Overview

Developed by Kubota Corporation, KATR (Kubota All-Terrain Robot) is a four-wheeled robotic platform with hydraulically actuated legs that adjust in real-time to maintain a level cargo deck on uneven terrain. Launched in 2024, it received the CES® 2025 Innovation Awards' "Best of Innovation" in Industrial Equipment & Machinery.

Designed for agriculture, construction, forestry, and disaster relief, it excels in areas where traditional machinery struggles. The innovative design combines the stability of tracked vehicles with the agility of legged robots, making it perfect for precision farming and challenging terrain operations where maintaining a level platform is crucial for cargo stability and operational efficiency.

Technical Specifications

Payload Capacity
Up to 240–284 kg (approximately 530 lb)
Wheel & Leg System
Dual-wheel per leg; hydraulic legs adjust to terrain to keep the deck level
Powertrain
Available with either combustion engine or electric drive (electric variant in development)
Size & Compactness
Small enough to fit into a light pickup truck bed or commercial minivan
Control Modes
Operable via onboard controller or remote control
Stability System
Proprietary real-time algorithm and sensor suite dynamically maintain level cargo platform on slopes
Steering
Pivot-turn capability enables operation in tight spaces
Terrain Capability
Handles slopes, uneven ground, and challenging agricultural environments

Capabilities

Level Cargo Deck: Hydraulic leg adjustments handle slopes and uneven ground seamlessly, maintaining stability for sensitive cargo.
Robust Multi-Terrain Mobility: Four-wheel drive, each on independent hydraulic legs, allow traversing rugged off-road environments.
Versatile Deployment: Supports agriculture, forestry, construction, and disaster relief operations across diverse environments.
Modular Platform: Cargo deck can carry tools, sensors, or even serve as a working platform for various applications.
Flexible Power Options: Users can choose combustion or full-electric variant to match use case and sustainability goals.

Applications

Agriculture: Especially effective in hilly or terraced farms, orchards, and vineyards; modular attachments (sprayers, sensors) aid in precision farming.
Construction & Forestry: Transport materials and operate equipment on steep, uneven terrain where traditional vehicles cannot safely operate.
Disaster Response: Delivers supplies over rubble or landslide-affected areas where wheeled vehicles struggle to maintain stability.
Research & Development: Acts as a scalable platform for experimenting with attachments (e.g., pruners, imagers, robotic arms).
Vineyard Operations: Perfect for navigating between vine rows and carrying harvesting equipment on slopes with precision.
